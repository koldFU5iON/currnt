## Overview

Echo is a playtesting and feedback intelligence program built to solve a specific problem: playtesting sessions generate enormous amounts of qualitative feedback that studios struggle to process, prioritise, and act on in time to matter.

Echo uses AI to ingest raw session notes, tester comments, and structured survey data, then generates studio-ready reports that surface patterns, prioritise issues by severity and frequency, and flag outliers that might otherwise be missed in the noise.

## My Role

Conceived, designed, and built the full system — from the data collection templates through to the AI processing pipeline and the report format that studios actually read. Worked directly with internal studios to iterate on what useful output looked like. Managed the rollout across the publishing portfolio and trained the teams who use it.

## Outcome

Turned playtesting from a bottleneck into a fast feedback loop. Studios now get structured, prioritised reports within hours of a session rather than days. The system has influenced production decisions on multiple titles and is considered core infrastructure by the studios that use it.
