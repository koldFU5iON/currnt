# Writing Rules

These rules apply to all AI-generated writing in this app.

## Voice and tone
- Write in first person ("I led…", not "the candidate led…")
- Be direct. Avoid corporate filler: "leverage", "synergize", "passionate", "dynamic", "results-driven", "proactive", "detail-oriented", "stakeholders"
- Vary sentence openings. Don't start three sentences in a row with "I"
- Prefer specific over generic: name technologies, team sizes, business outcomes

## Voice anchors

These are examples of the right register. Use them as a positive target, not just the prohibitions above.

| Weak (cut) | Strong (use) |
|------------|--------------|
| "Significantly scaled the program." | "Took a global creator program from 40 to 5,000+ active relationships in 18 months." |
| "Gained internal buy-in through demonstrated value." | "Adopted with no mandate." |
| "Led a high-profile pan-European event." | "Owned end-to-end delivery across 8 territories: event planning, budget distribution, talent prep, and media strategy." |
| "Supported commercial conversations." | "Embedded alongside the sales team to surface upsell opportunities with high-value studio accounts." |
| "Worked cross-functionally with legal and brand teams." | "Built cross-functional alignment with Legal and Brand on asset and consent workflows, cutting turnaround from weeks to days." |

The pattern: short declarative, metric-led, no hedge. Name the specific scope. Let the outcome speak.

## Accuracy
- Never invent experience, skills, or outcomes not present in the provided profile data
- If the profile lacks detail for a section, omit it or flag the gap. Don't fill with assumption.
- Do not inflate scope or seniority beyond what the data supports

## Formatting
- No em-dashes (—). Use commas, colons, or a new sentence instead
- No bullet points in prose sections (summaries, cover letters)
- Achievement bullets: lead with an action verb, include a measurable outcome where data exists
